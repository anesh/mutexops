FAQ

Common Questions.

How do you stop the agent from doing something it shouldn't? +

Hard constraints, not soft prompts. The LLM can only invoke registered skills, each skill enforces RBAC at the API layer before doing anything, and high-risk actions require a second factor (e.g. an SSO push) regardless of what the LLM proposes. If a request doesn't map cleanly to a skill, the agent says so and escalates to a human.

Which LLM and voice models do you use? +

Pluggable. We have reference configs for Claude (via Bedrock or Anthropic API), OpenAI, and Azure OpenAI for the orchestration LLM, plus the corresponding voice models. For regulated environments we support Bedrock and Azure OpenAI exclusively so calls never leave your cloud boundary.

Can callers be authenticated without a separate device? +

For low-risk skills (status lookups, ticket info), yes — via voiceprint or knowledge-based questions. For anything that mutates state (password reset, MFA reset, ticket creation as another user), we require an SSO push or OTP on a registered device. We don't unlock accounts on the basis of voice alone.

How does this integrate with our existing IVR? +

Two common patterns. Front-end: the agent answers first, deflects what it can, transfers to your existing queue otherwise. Back-end: your IVR routes specific menu options to the agent SIP endpoint. Both are supported; the right one depends on whether you want to deflect by intent or by menu.

Where do transcripts and recordings live? +

In your storage. Transcripts and audio land in your S3 / Azure Blob / GCS bucket with your retention and KMS keys. Structured action logs go to your SIEM. MutexOps doesn't retain call media on our side.
